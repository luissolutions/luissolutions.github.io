Steps to Obtain a Business License in South Carolina

  1. Choose a Business Name

    Use the Business Name Search tool on the SC Secretary of State website to check the availability of your desired business name. Ensure it complies with South Carolina naming requirements.

  2. Determine Your Business Structure

    Decide on the type of business entity you want to establish (e.g., sole proprietorship, corporation, LLC, partnership, etc.).

  3. Register Your Business Entity

    File the necessary paperwork with the SC Secretary of State. You can use the Business Entities Online system to file online. This step will officially establish your business entity in the state.

  4. Obtain an Employer Identification Number (EIN)

    If your business requires an EIN (most businesses do), apply for one through the IRS website. You'll need an EIN if you have employees, operate as a corporation or partnership, or have a Keogh plan.

  5. Determine Local Licensing Requirements

    Research the specific licensing and permit requirements for your business based on its location in South Carolina. These requirements can vary by city or county.

  6. Apply for a Business License

    Contact the appropriate local government office to obtain the necessary business license application forms. This could be your city or county government office, depending on where your business is located.

  7. Complete the Application

    Fill out the business license application form, providing all required information and documentation. Be prepared to provide your business name, EIN, contact information, and details about your business activities.

  8. Pay the License Fee

    Fee details are available on the South Carolina Department of Revenue site.

  9. Submit the Application

    Submit your completed application and payment to the local government office responsible for issuing business licenses. This may be done in person or through an online portal, depending on the local procedures.

  10. Wait for Approval

    Once approved, you will receive a business license. Display this license prominently at your business location as required by local regulations.
