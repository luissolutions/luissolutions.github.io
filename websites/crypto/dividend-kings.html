Why Dividend Kings?

Dividend Kings are companies with 50+ years of consecutive dividend increases. They are not high-risk, high-yield plays. They are slow, steady, reliable income machines.

  • Stability across market cycles
  • Strong, durable cash flows
  • Recession-resistant demand
  • Predictable, compounding income
  • Lower volatility than the broader market

Dividend Kings historically grow dividends 5%–10% per year — which compounds income even if your contributions slow down.

Tracking Progress

Three metrics tell you exactly where you stand in the system.

Portfolio Yield

Total annual dividends ÷ total portfolio value. Tells you your current income rate.

Annual Income

Sum of all dividends paid in the trailing 12 months. The number you're building toward $10,000.

Dividend Growth Rate

How fast your income increases year-over-year without adding new capital. Kings average 5%–10%/yr.

Reinvesting Dividends (DRIP)

Reinvesting dividends (Dividend Reinvestment Plan) is the core accelerant of this system.

  • Increases share count automatically — no action required
  • Accelerates compounding: more shares â†’ more dividends â†’ more shares
  • Can reduce time to reach $10k/yr by 30–40% compared to taking dividends as cash

DRIP is the default until you hit $10,000/year. Then you turn it off.

Transition to Cash Flow Mode

Once annual income reaches $10,000:

  1. Turn off DRIP
  2. Route dividends to your cash account
  3. Use dividends as passive income
  4. Continue adding capital if you want to grow beyond $10k

This creates a self-sustaining passive-income engine — the portfolio produces income without you touching the principal.
