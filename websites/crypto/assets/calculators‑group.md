# Calculators & Tools (Educational)  
A collection of simple, educational frameworks for understanding savings growth, retirement accounts, capital gains, small‑venture profit modeling, and dividend income projections.

---

# 1. Savings Growth Planner (Educational)

This planner helps estimate how savings grow over time.

## Key Variables
- Monthly contribution  
- Annual interest rate  
- Time horizon  
- Compounding frequency  

## Example Formula
Future Value = Contribution × (((1 + r)^n − 1) / r)

Where:
- r = monthly interest rate  
- n = number of months  

## Use Cases
- Emergency fund planning  
- Short‑term savings goals  
- Building a cash buffer  

---

# 2. Roth vs Traditional Comparison (Educational)

This tool helps compare the tax differences between Roth and Traditional retirement accounts.

## Roth IRA
- Contributions are after‑tax  
- Growth is tax‑free  
- Withdrawals in retirement are tax‑free  

## Traditional IRA
- Contributions may be tax‑deductible  
- Growth is tax‑deferred  
- Withdrawals are taxed as income  

## Comparison Factors
- Current tax bracket  
- Expected future tax bracket  
- Time horizon  
- Income level  

---

# 3. Capital Gains Estimator (Educational)

This estimator helps understand how capital gains taxes work.

## Key Concepts
- Short‑term gains (held < 1 year)  
- Long‑term gains (held ≥ 1 year)  
- Cost basis  
- Sale price  
- Tax bracket  

## Example Calculation
Capital Gain = Sale Price − Cost Basis

Tax Owed = Capital Gain × Applicable Tax Rate

## Use Cases
- Planning sales in taxable accounts  
- Understanding tax impact  
- Timing long‑term vs short‑term gains  

---

# 4. Small Venture Profit Estimator (Educational)

This estimator helps model small business or side‑venture profitability.

## Key Inputs
- Revenue  
- Cost of goods sold  
- Operating expenses  
- Taxes  
- One‑time costs  

## Example Formula
Profit = Revenue − Expenses − Taxes

## Use Cases
- Small online shops  
- Freelance work  
- Local service businesses  
- Early‑stage planning  

---

# 5. Dividend Income Projector (DRIP)

This tool helps estimate future dividend income with reinvestment.

## Key Variables
- Portfolio value  
- Dividend yield  
- Dividend growth rate  
- Contribution rate  
- Reinvestment (DRIP)  

## Basic Formula
Annual Income = Portfolio Value × Dividend Yield

## With Growth
Future Income = Current Income × (1 + Dividend Growth Rate)^Years

## DRIP Effect
Reinvested dividends increase:
- Share count  
- Future dividends  
- Compounding speed  

## Use Cases
- Long‑term dividend planning  
- Passive income projections  
- Retirement income modeling  

---

# 6. Summary

These educational tools help users understand:
- How savings grow  
- How retirement accounts differ  
- How capital gains taxes work  
- How small ventures generate profit  
- How dividends compound over time  

They are frameworks for learning and planning, not financial advice.
