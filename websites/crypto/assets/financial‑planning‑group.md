# Financial Planning & Quick‑Start Guides  
Educational frameworks for savings, retirement accounts, real estate, and long‑term investing.

---

# 1. Short‑Term Savings vs Long‑Term Investing

Short‑term savings and long‑term investing serve different purposes.

## Short‑Term Savings (0–24 months)
Used for:
- Emergency fund  
- Car repairs  
- Travel  
- Small purchases  
- Short‑term goals  

Characteristics:
- Low risk  
- Low return  
- High liquidity  

Common vehicles:
- High‑yield savings accounts  
- Money market accounts  
- Short‑term CDs  

## Long‑Term Investing (3+ years)
Used for:
- Retirement  
- Wealth building  
- Passive income  
- Long‑term goals  

Characteristics:
- Higher risk  
- Higher return potential  
- Long time horizon  

Common vehicles:
- Index funds  
- ETFs  
- Dividend stocks  
- Retirement accounts  

---

# 2. Roth IRA Strategy (Educational)

A Roth IRA is a retirement account where contributions are made with after‑tax dollars.

## Key Features
- Tax‑free growth  
- Tax‑free withdrawals in retirement  
- No required minimum distributions  
- Contribution limits apply  

## When Roth IRA is useful
- Long time horizon  
- Expect higher income later  
- Want tax‑free retirement income  

## Contribution Strategy
- Contribute consistently  
- Automate monthly deposits  
- Use broad, diversified investments  
- Avoid frequent trading  

---

# 3. Real Estate Strategy (Owner‑Occupant & Beyond)

Real estate can complement long‑term investing.

## Owner‑Occupant Strategy
- Live in the property  
- Build equity over time  
- Lower interest rates  
- Potential tax benefits  

## Rental Strategy (Educational)
- Rental income  
- Appreciation  
- Leverage through financing  

## Key Considerations
- Maintenance costs  
- Property taxes  
- Vacancy risk  
- Cash flow analysis  

---

# 4. Index Funds vs ETFs (Educational)

Index funds and ETFs are both diversified investment vehicles.

## Index Funds
- Trade once per day  
- Often used in retirement accounts  
- Long‑term focused  

## ETFs
- Trade throughout the day  
- Often lower expense ratios  
- More flexible  

## Similarities
- Diversification  
- Low cost  
- Long‑term growth potential  

---

# 5. Account Types & Tax Basics (Educational)

Understanding account types helps with long‑term planning.

## Taxable Brokerage Account
- No contribution limits  
- Capital gains taxes apply  
- Flexible withdrawals  

## Traditional IRA
- Pre‑tax contributions  
- Tax‑deferred growth  
- Taxed on withdrawal  

## Roth IRA
- After‑tax contributions  
- Tax‑free growth  
- Tax‑free withdrawals  

## 401(k)
- Employer‑sponsored  
- Pre‑tax or Roth options  
- Higher contribution limits  

---

# 6. Short‑Term Cash & Yield Options (Educational)

For short‑term goals, stability matters more than return.

## Common Options
- High‑yield savings  
- Money market funds  
- Short‑term treasuries  
- CDs  

## Characteristics
- Low volatility  
- High liquidity  
- Predictable returns  

---

# 7. Summary

Short‑term savings protect you.  
Long‑term investing grows you.  
Retirement accounts provide tax advantages.  
Real estate adds diversification.  
Index funds and ETFs offer simplicity.  

Together, these tools form a **complete financial foundation** for long‑term wealth building.
